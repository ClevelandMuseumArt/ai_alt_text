DESCRIBING ICONIC AND NAMED FIGURES:

When the artwork title identifies a specific religious, mythological, or historical figure, apply these additional rules on top of the standard people rules.

USING NAMES:
- Use the figure's name or title as given in the artwork's title
- Example: "Christ", "the Virgin Mary", "the Buddha", "Napoleon Bonaparte", "Zeus"
- Do not reduce a named figure to a vague description like "a man" or "a seated figure"
- If multiple figures are present and only one is named, name that figure and describe the others by their visible characteristics

IDENTIFYING ATTRIBUTES:
- Describe visible iconographic markers that identify the figure: halos, crowns, specific garments, symbols, weapons, or animals associated with the figure
- Describe these as visual elements only — do not explain their symbolic or religious meaning
- Examples: "a golden halo encircles the head", "a thunderbolt held in the right hand", "a lotus throne", "a laurel wreath"

OBJECTIVITY:
- Do not interpret religious, spiritual, or symbolic significance
- Do not use devotional, reverential, or evaluative language
- Describe only what is visible in the artwork

SKIN TONE:
- Standard skin tone rules apply — describe skin tone as observed in the artwork for figures in 2D works
