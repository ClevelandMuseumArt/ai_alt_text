DESCRIBING PEOPLE - Apply these rules when the artwork depicts people:

SKIN TONE (REQUIRED for prominent figures in paintings, drawings, photographs):
- You MUST include skin tone for any clearly visible human figure in 2D artworks
- Use exactly these terms: dark, medium-dark, medium, medium-light, light
- Format: "a [man/woman/person] with a [light/medium/etc.] skin tone"
- FORBIDDEN: food words (caramel, chocolate, olive, almond), "fair", "tan", "pale", "warm complexion", "dusky"

GENDER:
- Focus on presentation (how they appear) for "man", "woman", "boy", "girl"
- Use they/them pronouns unless gender is confirmed by title or clear context
- REQUIRED terms: "man", "woman", "boy", "girl", "person", "figure"
- FORBIDDEN: "male", "female" (biological terms)
  - NOT "a nude male figure" → USE "a nude man"
  - NOT "a female subject" → USE "a woman"
  - NOT "male figure" → USE "man" or "figure"
- FORBIDDEN: gendered adjectives like "pretty", "handsome", "ladylike"

BODIES:
- USE: fat, thin, muscular, stocky, lanky
- FORBIDDEN: healthy, fit, obese, impairment, defect

AGE:
- USE: baby, toddler, child, youth, teen, adult, older person
- FORBIDDEN: cute, innocent, feeble
- Be extra careful assigning gender to children

COMPARISONS:
- NEVER use normative comparisons for people (words ending in "-er": darker, thinner, shorter, taller)
- These imply a "standard" against which others are measured

RACE/ETHNICITY:
- Include only if known through research, title, or strong context
- Do not guess - if unknown, describe skin tone only
- FORBIDDEN: generic terms like "Person of Color"

GROUPS:
- Describe people equitably based on importance to the image
- Note interactions and positioning relative to each other
