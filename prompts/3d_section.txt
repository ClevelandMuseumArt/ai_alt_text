DESCRIBING 3D OBJECTS:

IGNORE STUDIO/MUSEUM SETUP:
- Do NOT describe the museum or studio background behind 3D objects (gray backdrop, gradient, etc.)
- Do NOT mention lighting setups, shadows from photography, or how the object is photographed
- Do NOT use terms like "studio shot", "full shot", "against a gray background"
- Focus ONLY on the object itself and any integral base/pedestal that is part of the artwork

CORE SHAPE FIRST:
- Immediately identify what the object is or represents
- Examples: "A teapot-shaped vessel", "A standing figure", "Three stacked spheres"

MATERIALS AND SURFACE:
- State material: bronze, marble, ceramic, wood, gold, jade
- Describe surface finish: polished, matte, rough, textured, patinated
- Note color variations in the material

SPATIAL LANGUAGE:
- Use directional terms: front, back, left side, right side, top, bottom
- Describe depth: projecting forward, receding, relief
- Note how forms relate in space

THREE-DIMENSIONALITY:
- Describe how forms project or recede
- Note hollows, cavities, openings
- Describe the silhouette from the primary viewing angle

LIGHT AND SURFACE:
- Note how light interacts: reflective, absorbing, creating shadows
- Describe highlights and areas of shadow

CONDITION AND AGE:
- Note visible wear, patina, cracks, repairs
- Describe surface characteristics from age: oxidation, discoloration

BASE AND MOUNTING:
- Describe pedestals, bases, or display mounts if visible
- Note if the object stands freely or requires support

PEOPLE:
- Do not describe skin tone if the sculpture or material of the object dominates the color scheme
