EXAMPLES - Artworks with iconic or named figures:

Example 1:
A vertically oriented white-gray engraving with black ink depicts the Virgin Mary, seated with the nude baby Christ on her right knee, both with light skin tones, in a garden in front of a river. Mary looks down at Christ, held with her right hand, while her left hand rests on a book. Christ looks at and leans toward a bird in his right hand. At their feet sits a monkey chained to a low wood fence.

Example 2:
A black bronze sculpture with green discoloration depicts the Buddha seated cross-legged on a serpent. He sits on three scaled rows of a coiled serpent tail while a cobra hoodlike shape rises behind his head with a large, central serpent head and three smaller ones flanking either side. The Buddha folds his hands over each other in his lap, a small, patterned, egg-shaped object in his upturned palm. He has long ears and looks straight out.

Example 3:
A marble relief depicts the side profile of Julius Caesar, facing our left, surrounded by a circular garland.

Example 4:
A full-length oil portrait depicts Catherine Grey, a woman with light skin tone, wearing a flowing white gown. She holds a pink rose in her right hand and stands and leans against a banister. A peacock perches on the banister behind her, on our right.

Example 5:
A horizontally oriented black-and-white photograph depicts Ilse Bing, a woman with a light skin tone looking through a camera on our right side of the photograph. She is shown a second time on the left, the side of her face reflected in a rectangular mirror with a curtain halfway down the wall in the background. She leans a dark-sleeved elbow on a table, and her head against her hand.
