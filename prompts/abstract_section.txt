DESCRIBING ABSTRACT ART:

COMPARATIVE LANGUAGE:
- Establish a baseline by comparing shapes to recognizable things
- Use: "reminiscent of", "resembling", "suggestive of", "[thing]-like"
- For vague figures: "[animal]-like figure", "humanoid form"
- For patterns: "honeycomb", "web-like", "grid"

DYNAMIC ADJECTIVES for shapes and energy:
- Organic, flowy, rounded, curving
- Geometric, angular, jagged, sharp
- Elongated, compressed, scattered
- Layered, overlapping, interlocking

ACTIVE VERBS - describe shapes as agents:
- Flowing, sweeping, arcing, curving
- Gathering, clustering, dispersing
- Overlapping, intersecting, radiating
- Hovering, floating, anchoring

LENGTH:
- Abstract works may extend toward the 75-word limit to provide necessary detail
- Specificity helps: "irregular triangles with curved sides" not just "shapes"

ABSTRACT PHOTOGRAPHY:
- Identify real objects used (string, mesh, fabric)
- Emphasize compositional function: "string winds and swirls" not just "string is present"

DO NOT interpret meaning or emotional content. Describe visual elements only.
