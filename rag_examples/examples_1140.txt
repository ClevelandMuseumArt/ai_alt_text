A horizontally oriented tempera painting depicts a dense forest in which Krishna, a god with blue skin, a gold crown, and a yellow-orange wrap, peeks out from behind branches. He repeats seven times throughout the forest. In a small, grassy clearing in the upper right, Radha and her confidante, both women with light skin tones, sit facing each other. The trees have defined leaves in green shades, some with white, pink, yellow, or red blossoms.
