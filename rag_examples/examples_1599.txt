A set of forty-eight watercolor portraits arranged in four rows of twelve each depict a portrait of a woman from the shoulders up. Positioned angled in all directions, some looking at us, others not, their skin tones range from light to medium-dark. The colorful watercolors loosely render each woman, showing through to the beige paper at points. Emma Amos's self-portrait features in the upper right corner and her daughter India's portrait the upper left.
