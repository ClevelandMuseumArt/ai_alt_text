A gilded bronze sculpture depicts the standing Avalokiteshvara, an enlightened being with six arms and eleven heads standing with his hips swinging to his right. His heads stack on top of each other in a pyramidal shape, with three rows of three heads and two rows of singular heads, the ascending heads a third the size of those in the first row. A fishbone-patterned loop snakes around his neck and hangs down to his shins.
