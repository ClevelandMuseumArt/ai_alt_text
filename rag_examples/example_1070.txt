A brown limestone sculpture depicts Sakyamuni Buddha flanked by two deities half his size standing in front of a mandorla, a pointed oval halo. He and the deities wear robes draped in "U"-shaped folds, hold out their hands, and have faint smiles with their hair pulled up in buns, save for the deity on our right whose head has broken off. A halo and circle with foliage patterns surrounds Sakyamuni's head, transitioning to flame-like patterns.
