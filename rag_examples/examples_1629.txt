A gold, insect-like pendant has a beetle-shaped body with tendril-like extensions protruding like legs from either side of it. A horizontal band divides the body into an upper third and lower two-thirds. In the upper third, spheres suggest eyes, a vertical ridge sticking up between them. Two protrusions along the edge suggest pincers. The lower two-thirds are marked solely by a vertical line dividing the body evenly in two.
