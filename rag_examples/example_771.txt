A wakahuia, a cylindrical, dark brown wood shallow box, features a line running horizontally across the center where the lid meets the base. It is flanked on either side by stylized figures' heads. Intersecting straight-lined and spiral designs completely cover the box, either solid lines or rows of squat diamond-shapes. These lines also patterns the figures' faces, the top of their heads flat and their eyes made from subtle yellow-green colored rings.
